Core Thesis

文明不是静态秩序，而是 surplus 的创造与捕获。

The central question is not who claims moral authority. The central question is who creates new capacity, who controls access, who captures the surplus, and who opens the next frontier.

The Cycle

Every frontier creates both Builders and future gatekeepers.

Builders turn disorder into capability. But successful Builders accumulate power, and power attracts rent. The civilizational cycle begins when a frontier opens and ends when its surplus becomes institutional capture.

  1. New frontier appears
  2. Builders create surplus
  3. Takers capture gates
  4. Institutions harden
  5. Crisis exposes old rent
  6. New Builders restart expansion

America

Cold War victory removed the external constraint.

The transnational elite system once served American power. After 1991, it increasingly treated America itself as a governance platform: globalized, financialized, managed, and morally audited by institutions that were not directly accountable to citizens.

Access Control

Education, funding, media legitimacy, international careers, legal norms, platform rules, and capital standards.

Moral Legitimacy

The power to define what counts as open, inclusive, democratic, professional, and civilized.

Rent Conversion

Surplus becomes monopoly, bureaucracy, credential capture, platform dependency, or ideological gatekeeping.

New Frontier

AI, energy, robotics, compute, and space are rewriting the production function.

The decisive question is not whether these technologies become powerful. The decisive question is whether they lower the cost of building or become the next rent stack.

The final standard is simple.

The system that gives the most Builders real access to new frontiers is the system most likely to lead human civilization.
